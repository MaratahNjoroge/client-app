The Manifesto

The change that we need:

After 60 years of tribal, corrupt, unprincipled, incompetent and short sighted selfish leadership, August 9, 2022 is the time for fresh, new, honest, principled, competent and visionary leadership that will deliver on jobs, housing, education, healthcare, business, end corruption, end injustice and end tribalism. Chama Cha Mimi Na Wewe is an all inclusive political party that has been formed to address the challenges that face Kenya and her citizens. We plan to play our part in coming up with solutions that can lead to a better Kenya.

Part of our agenda includes;

  1. Wanjiku Bill

    2. All elected officials to be put on a performance contract whereby their monthly salary will be pegged on maintaining unemployment rate below 5%.

  2. Creation of employment for all Kenyans

    • Lay infrastructure across Kenya i.e. piped water and sewerage, mixture of murram and tarmac roads, fiber and electricity. This will cut down the cost of construction for public and public developers leading to the construction of houses, offices, business and leisure facilities across the country that will result in employment creation.
    • Develop agri-business through provision of irrigation, affordable loans, research and easy access to markets.
    • Facilitate construction of at least 0.5 million houses annually by adapting reliable and affordable technology.
    • Set up economic and industrial zones at county levels to provide an enabling environment for manufacturing.
    • Support SME’s through the provision of affordable loans.

  3. Facilitate the construction of 0.5 million houses yearly.
  4. A lean, professional, efficient, and inclusive civil service.
  5. Transparency, accountability, and public participation in public affairs.

    • SMS is one of the most cost-effective, efficient, and inclusive means of communication. To leverage this, citizens will have an opportunity to have their voices heard in public affairs through SMS.
      All decisions whether legislative or executive that has been debated upon will finally be approved by the public through SMS voting.
      Every Friday of every week, matters that need to be voted on whether from the legislature or the executive will be published and members of the public will vote on them. Eventually, this will also include elections. Electing leaders and recalling non-performing leaders will be as simple and cost-effective as sending an SMS.
      This will cut down on cost, increase public participation and hold elected leaders to account. My Tax, My Voice.

  6. End corruption in public service.

    • All public institutions and officials to give a daily account of their expenditure on an online platform accessible to the public.
    • All public institutions to give a daily account of their suppliers i.e. Name, Owners, History, Tax Status, etc.
    • Strengthen the offices of the Auditor-General, Ethics and Anti Corruption Commission and the CID enable them to carry regular audits/investigations on public institutions and public officials, at intervals of at least once monthly.
    • Strengthen the Office of the Director of Public prosecution to be able to carry out successful prosecutions
    • All public officials declare their wealth on a monthly basis. Unlimited lifestyle audits to be conducted whenever there is suspicion of corruption
    • Set up a free mobile-based system for reporting corruption.
    • Impose stiffer penalties on corruption offenses.

What about the cabinet, you may ask

Our lead cabinet will be composed of;

  1. Presidency
  2. Attorney General
    • Revise and improve on the current constitution to address challenges that have arisen since its passage.
    • Address historical injustices through the implementation of the TJRC report
  3. Treasury
    • 65 % of the budget to go to development expenditure, 35 % of the budget to go to recurrent expenditure.
    • For efficiency and cost effectiveness, have one identity document for identification, PIN, NSSF, NHIF, Voters Card, driving licence, passport etc.
    • Ensure all citizens, foreigners and businesses pay taxes
    • Tax returns for all citizens, foreigners and businesses to be filed monthly on mobile phone platforms (App or SMS).
    • All past uncollected taxes including from individuals who have died, to be collected.
    • KRA to give daily records of taxes collected
    • KRA to give monthly reports on useful data e.g. employment/unemployment situation in the country, income levels etc.
  4. Land, urban planning & Housing.
    • Digitize all land records to ensure land transactions are transparent and credible.
    • Conduct a master plan for all counties.
    • Reverse rural-urban migration by urbanizing the rural areas.
    • Create serviced land (piped water, sewerage, roads etc.) in all counties
  5. Environment, Water, Sanitation and Agriculture
    • Put up solid waste management and recycling units at county levels.
    • Set up a water resource management system at county levels i.e. tapping of water, provision of piped water and recycling of used water.
    • Set up an efficient irrigation network in areas where food and other crops are being produced.
    • Ensure full support to agricultural industries.
  6. Health
    • Set up an efficient public health system by learning from successful countries like United Kingdom, Cuba and India.
  7. Education
    • Set up a relevant and efficient public education system by learning and adapting from successful countries like Germany and Japan.
  8. Energy & ICT
    • Reduce cost, ensure inclusiveness and enhance efficiency in energy and ICT.
  9. Roads, Transport and Public Works
    • Lay infrastructure at county levels.
  10. Defense & Internal Security.
    • Cut costs and enhance efficiency in internal and external security.
    • Reduce crime through petitions in prayers.
    • Incorporating citizens in security systems.
    • Eliminating poverty and creating employment.
    • Planned and controlled real estate developments.
    • Maximizing on technology e.g. CCTV.
    • Propagate the setting up of a continent wide defence force with the ability to respond to internal and external threats so as to enhance efficiency and reduce costs.
  11. Devolution
    • Reverse rural-urban migration by urbanizing the rural areas.
  12. Arts, Sport and Culture

  13. Foreign Affairs

    14. Champion unification of Africa into one country with 3 levels of government i.e. Federal, State and County. This will;

    • Reduce inefficiencies and maximize potential.
    • Attract increased internal and external investments.
    • Create a big market with economic potential comparable to the USA, China and India
  14. Trade, Tourism and Wildlife
    • Maximize on all potential available in trade, tourism and wildlife.
    • Scientific innovations resulting in products that have a global demand have led to rapid developments in countries like USA, Germany, Japan, Korea etc. In Kenya we have talented individuals who are capable of coming up with new inventions but what has been lacking is funding for research and developments. This is evident in the annual secondary school’s science congress and scientific exhibitions in our colleges and universities. Our government will set up a 1 billion shillings research and development fund. Annual science fairs open to all will be held and the top 100 inventions with likelihood of producing usable products and in the process create employment, will receive a grant of Ksh 10 million each. Inventors will be assisted to solicit additional funds from investors and banks.
  15. Labour

    16. Learn from progressive countries so as to come with a sustainable, responsive, fair and economically sound human resource framework.
